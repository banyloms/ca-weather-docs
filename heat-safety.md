## Heat Safety Guidelines (NWS)

- **Stay hydrated**: Drink plenty of fluids and avoid alcohol.
- **Seek shade & air‑conditioning**: Remain in cool environments, especially during peak heat hours.
- **Check on vulnerable populations**: Young children, infants, older adults, pregnant women, and people with chronic medical conditions are at higher risk.
- **Never leave children, pets, or anyone in a parked car** – temperatures can become lethal within minutes.
- **Dress appropriately**: Wear lightweight, loose‑fitting clothing and a wide‑brimmed hat.
- **Limit strenuous activity**: Schedule outdoor work for early morning or evening; take frequent breaks.
- **Recognize heat‑related illness symptoms**: Heat exhaustion (heavy sweating, weakness, dizziness) and heat stroke (high body temperature, confusion, loss of consciousness) require immediate medical attention.
- **Use sun protection**: Apply sunscreen, sunglasses, and limit direct sun exposure.
- **Educate and prepare**: Share safety information and have a plan for heat emergencies.
